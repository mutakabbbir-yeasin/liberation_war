People's Republic of Bangladesh

Bangladesh's history spans ancient kingdoms (Mauryan, Gupta, Pala) and Islamic Sultanates, becoming the wealthy Bengal province of the Mughal Empire before British conquest in 1757. Partition in 1947 made it East Pakistan, but cultural differences and political neglect led to the Language Movement (1952) and Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's Six-Point demands. This culminated in the 1971 Liberation War, where East Pakistan fought West Pakistan, achieving independence as Bangladesh on December 16, 1971, after a brutal conflict.
Path to Independence (1971): Bengalis faced political, economic, and cultural suppression in Pakistan. The 1952 Language Movement for Bangla recognition was a key moment. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's 1966 Six-Point Program demanded autonomy. After the 1970 election victory by his Awami League was blocked, the Pakistani military launched a brutal crackdown in March 1971. Liberation War & Birth of Bangladesh (1971): A nine-month Liberation War ensued, with Bengalis fighting for freedom. The war ended on December 16, 1971, with the surrender of Pakistani forces, establishing Bangladesh as an independent nation.
